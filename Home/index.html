About

Dharmapuri District, located in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, spans 4,532 square kilometers, bordering Karnataka, Krishnagiri, Salem, and Tiruvannamalai districts, with its headquarters in Dharmapuri town, 130 km from Bangalore and 300 km from Chennai. The region boasts rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and scenic beauty, with major attractions like Hogenakkal Falls, a 180-foot waterfall on the Kaveri River, and Theerthamalai's ancient Shiva temple, along with other historical sites like the Dharmapuri Fort and Pennagaram. Agriculture drives the economy, with paddy, sugarcane, and groundnut as primary crops, and Dharmapuri is renowned for mango and turmeric production, earning it the nickname "Turmeric City." The district's population of approximately 1.5 million predominantly speaks Tamil, with diverse communities coexisting harmoniously. Education and healthcare infrastructure have improved significantly, with government and private institutions offering quality services. Dharmapuri continues to grow, balancing industrialization and cultural preservation, with initiatives focusing on tourism development, infrastructure upgrades, and social welfare programs. The district's historical significance, natural wonders, and cultural festivals, like the Dharmapuri Mariamman Temple Car Festival, attract tourists and showcase its unique identity. With its blend of tradition and modernity, Dharmapuri District strives for sustainable growth and prosperity, leveraging its strengths in agriculture, tourism, and human resources to create a brighter future for its residents, while also addressing challenges like water scarcity and environmental conservation to ensure long-term development.
